## Summary of Principles

1. **Monon Series is not emergent.**  
   A *system of planemons* is not a larger planemon.  
   A *binary of stellamons* is not a single, larger stellamon.

2. **Modeling convenience â‰  ontological unity.**  
   In orbital mechanics it is **useful** to treat a binary as one object of combined mass.  
   Ontotypically, however, the **barycenter abstraction** is *not* a monon.

3. **Distinct fields of cohesion.**  
   - A monon has one **center of cohesion**, even if dynamic (e.g., a rotating planemon).  
   - A system has **multiple discrete centers**, even if mathematically reducible.  

> For instance, in a double-star stellamonic system it may be convenient to treat the two stellamons as a single mass centered on the barycenter of their orbits.  
> Yet this is only a **mathematical convenience** derived from system properties â€” not a **physical entity** in itself.
